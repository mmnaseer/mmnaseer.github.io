About

I am a dedicated PhD researcher at Teesside University International Business School, specializing in climate change, corporate social responsibility, and green finance. My research mainly explores how climate risks affect firm value, market volatility, and ESG performance. With a strong background in finance and econometrics, I have gained expertise in advanced analytical techniques to study these complex interactions.

Research interests

  • Climate risk exposure and firm outcomes
  • Corporate governance and ethics
  • ESG performance and disclosure
  • Asset pricing and volatility

Methods

  • Panel econometrics (FE/RE, quantile)
  • Textual analysis of earnings calls
  • PSM and robustness designs
  • High-dimensional fixed effects
