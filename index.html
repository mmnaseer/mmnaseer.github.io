About

I am a dedicated PhD researcher at Teesside University International Business School, specializing in climate change, corporate social responsibility, and green finance. My research mainly explores how climate risks affect firm value, market volatility, and ESG performance. With a strong background in finance and econometrics, I have gained expertise in advanced analytical techniques to study these complex interactions. Throughout my academic journey, I have received numerous awards for my focus on multidisciplinary research and for publishing in prestigious journals such as the International Review of Financial Analysis, Finance Research Letters, and Research in International Business and Finance. In addition to my academic work, I also have professional experience in financial management, where I have sharpened my skills in strategic planning and financial forecasting. (postdoc/lectureship/collaboration).
